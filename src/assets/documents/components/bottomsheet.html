Bottom Sheet
Bottom sheets are surfaces containing supplementary content that are anchored to the bottom of the screen. Bottom sheets slide up from the bottom of the screen to reveal more content.
Examples Examples
Bottom sheets are intended primarily as an interaction on mobile devices where they can be used as an alternative to dialogs and menus.

Best Practices Best Practices
Bottom sheets should:
  • Supporting: Bottom sheets contain content that supplements the screen’s primary UI region.
  • Flexible: Bottom sheets can display a wide variety of content and layouts.
  • Ergonomic: Bottom sheets are easy to reach on a mobile device.
Usage & Content Guidelines Content Guidelines
A bottom sheet is anchored to the bottom edge of the screen. Don’t inset all sides of a bottom sheet from screen edges. This can make it hard to see and allow it to be confused for other components, such as snackbars.
Use a visible scrim with modal bottom sheets to inform users they cannot interact with the rest of the screen. Don’t use an invisible scrim for modal bottom sheets. This can mislead users about their ability to interact with the rest of the screen.
